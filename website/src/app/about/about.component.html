Hester Strumpfer

Vision

My vision is to create art from my soul. I need to reflect my deep passion for art in every piece I create. I put much thought in my work. I design and create costumes for my models. I hand pick models and I have a fondness for Rasta people and people with curly hair. My art is a form of therapy for myself and by working through my own trauma, I hope to speak to my audience and bring hope to them as well as some of the models I use. My art is 100% original, as I style and art direct my compositions myself. I take my own photos for reference, but often use professional photographers where I feel my skills are lacking.

About

Since my childhood days, I have had an immense passion for the arts. I was always drawn to portraiture and did my first drawing at age 14. In my mid-twenties, I endeavoured to take part in a small solo, titled “Children of the World”, and drew children of different ethnic groups in a variety of mediums. A career in the sciences was brought to a halt by chronic fatigue. Only after undergoing severe trauma in my late 40's, did I once more pick up my art skills and enrol in further training in both painting and drawing. I also completed a three year course in Art Counselling, passing with a Cum Laude in 2018. I continue to hone the craft and passion that is art. I draw with an equal passion for charcoal and pastels and I paint primarily with oils, but also wine and ink.
